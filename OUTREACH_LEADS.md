# Outreach & Lead Sources — Building Compliance OS

Where to find prospective customers for the LL97 compliance product, organized by channel. Pair each source with the cold-email template (see below) and the one-page flyer (`flyer.html`).

## Target Customer Profile

- **Primary:** NYC building owners and property managers responsible for LL97 filings on buildings >25,000 sq ft.
- **Sweet spot:** portfolios of 1–50 buildings (matches our Pro and Portfolio tiers).
- **Buyer titles:** Owner / Principal, Director of Property Management, Director of Sustainability, Asset Manager, Compliance Manager, Operations Director.
- **Secondary (referral channel):** LL97 consultants, energy advisors, real estate attorneys, CPAs.

## Lead Sources

### 1. REBNY Member Firms
- **URL:** https://rebny.com/members
- **Who:** Real Estate Board of New York — owner, brokerage, and operator firms.
- **How to filter:** Owner-operators with NYC commercial or multifamily portfolios of 5–50 buildings.
- **Why it works:** REBNY members have the most exposure to LL97 penalties and the budget to act. Many have multiple LL97-eligible buildings.

### 2. BOMA New York
- **URL:** https://bomany.org
- **Who:** Building Owners and Managers Association of NY — commercial building managers and owners.
- **How to filter:** Member directory; focus on management firms running 10–30 buildings.
- **Why it works:** These are the exact people responsible for LL97 filings. Many actively shop for compliance workflow tools.

### 3. NYC DOB / ACRIS Public Records
- **URLs:**
  - ACRIS (property ownership): https://acris.nyc.gov
  - DOB BIS (Building Information System): https://a.bisa.nyc.gov
- **Who:** Public records of every NYC property owner.
- **How to filter:** Search by owner name, block/lot, or square footage. Pull owners with multiple buildings >25,000 sq ft.
- **Why it works:** Surfaces portfolio owners who don't appear in trade-association directories — including smaller LLCs and family offices that have no consultant relationship.

### 4. LinkedIn Search
- **Search filters to use:**
  - Title: "property manager" OR "asset manager" OR "director of operations" OR "compliance manager"
  - Location: New York City Metropolitan Area
  - Company size: 10–200 employees
  - Industry: Real Estate, Commercial Real Estate, Real Estate Investment, Leasing Non-residential Real Estate
  - Profile keywords: "portfolio," "multifamily," "commercial real estate," "LL97," "Local Law 97"
- **Why it works:** Decision-makers who sign compliance contracts are reachable on LinkedIn and respond to direct messages about regulatory deadlines.

### 5. LL97 Consultants & Energy Advisors (Referral Partners)
- **How to find:** Google and LinkedIn search "LL97 consultant NYC," "Local Law 97 compliance consultant," "energy auditor NYC."
- **Also:** NYC Accelerator partner list — https://nyc-accelerator.com
- **Approach:** Frame as a tool that handles the rote calculation work so they can focus on advisory/strategy. White-label or reseller framing converts well.
- **Why it works:** Independent consultants currently doing this manually become your distribution channel.

### 6. NYC HDC and Affordable-Housing Operators
- **URL:** https://www.nychdc.com (project database lists borrowers)
- **Also:** NYCHA-adjacent property operators
- **Who:** HDC-financed buildings >25,000 sq ft are subject to LL97. Affordable housing operators face the same penalties with tighter margins.
- **Why it works:** The cost-savings pitch is sharpest here — a $50K penalty hurts more in this segment than in luxury commercial.

### 7. Real Estate Attorneys & CPAs Serving NYC Owners
- **URLs:**
  - NY State Bar Association: https://nysba.org (real estate section)
  - NYSSCPA: https://www.nysscpa.org (real estate practice committees)
- **Who:** Trusted advisors who counsel building owners on regulatory risk every year.
- **Approach:** Position the product as something they can recommend to clients, not something that competes with their service.
- **Why it works:** Warm referrals from a trusted advisor convert at much higher rates than cold outreach.

## Outreach Cadence Recommendation

1. **Week 1–2:** Build a list of 200 prospects from the sources above (mix of direct buyers and referral partners).
2. **Week 3:** First cold email to direct-buyer list with the flyer attached.
3. **Week 4:** Follow-up email to non-responders. Different subject line, shorter body.
4. **Week 5:** LinkedIn connection request to non-responders, no pitch in the connect note.
5. **Week 6:** After connection accepts, send a one-line follow-up referencing the original email.
6. **Ongoing:** Separate slow-burn cadence for referral partners (consultants, attorneys, CPAs) — coffee chats, not pitches.

## Cold Email Template

Subject line options:
1. Your LL97 penalty for [Building Address] could be six figures
2. $268/ton — are you exposed under Local Law 97?
3. Most NYC owners don't know their LL97 number. Do you?

Body:

```
If your building is over 25,000 sq ft and exceeds its LL97 carbon limit,
the City charges $268 per ton — with no cap. For a mid-size multifamily
or commercial building, that penalty can land between $50,000 and
$150,000 per year, billed annually starting with the May 1 filing.

Most owners find out what they owe from a consultant — after paying
$5,000–$25,000 for the analysis.

We built Building Compliance OS to cut that down to 10 minutes per
building. Enter your utility data, get your exact exposure, run penalty
scenarios, and generate a filing-ready PDF report. First building is free.

Worth a 20-minute look? [Calendar link / website]

[Your Name]
[Your Company]

P.S. If you manage multiple buildings, the portfolio view handles up to
50 — one dashboard, one filing workflow.
```

## Tracking Suggestions

- Maintain the prospect list in a CRM (HubSpot free tier, Pipedrive, or even a Notion table) with: Source, Name, Title, Company, Buildings (estimated), Email, LinkedIn, Status, Last Contact.
- Track which source produces the highest reply and conversion rates after the first 100 sends — double down there.
- Save every "no" with a reason; revisit in 6 months when LL97 deadline pressure ramps up.
